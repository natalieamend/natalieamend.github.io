The dangers in Chicago's poor communities are deeper than the surface.
Many see Chicago as a dangerous place, mostly because of national coverage of the city's high homicide rate, gangs and gun violence occurring primarily in its poorer neighborhoods.
But poor neighborhoods in Chicago are in even more danger. According to the Chicago Department of Public Health, the city's five poorest communnity areas have a 60% higher chance of dying from all causes compared to the five least poor community areas. With homicide as the eighth-leading cause of death in the city, it's important to realize the bigger dangers that Chicago's poverished communities face.
