Shake or Stir?

The general rule of thumb for putting together a cocktail is the following:

  • Stir a cocktail when it is predominately made of spirits and/or very light mixers. An example would be a martini, which has gin and vermouth, or a negroni, which consists of gin, Campari and sweet vermouth.
  • Shake a cocktail when it has more flavorful and thicker mixtures, like fruit/citrus juices, simple syrup, eggs or dairy. A Daiquiri, for example, needs to be shaken so the lime juice and simple syrup blend together with the rum.
